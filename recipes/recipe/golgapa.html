GOLGAPPE

Panipuri is one of the most iconic street snacks in the Indian subcontinent, consisting of a small, deep-fried, hollow sphere (puri) that is filled with a flavorful mixture and dipped in tangy, spiced water. The stuffing typically includes mashed potatoes, chickpeas, onions, and various spices, while the "pani" (water) is usually a combination of a spicy mint-coriander blend and a sweet tamarind chutney. Designed to be eaten in a single bite, it provides an immediate burst of sweet, sour, and spicy flavors that vary depending on the vendor's unique recipe

While the core concept remains the same, panipuri is celebrated for its immense regional diversity, even changing names as one travels across India. In the North, it is famously known as Golgappa, often using semolina-based puris and jaljeera-flavored water. In West Bengal and Bihar, it is called Puchka, featuring larger shells and a sharper, spicier filling often mixed with mustard oil. Other regional variants include Gup Chup in Odisha, Pani ke Patashe in Uttar Pradesh, and Pakodi in parts of Gujarat, reflecting the unique culinary preferences and local ingredients of each state.

INGREDIENTS

  • Puri (hollow shells)
  • Spiced potato filling
  • Tamarind and mint water
  • Sweet chutney

STEPS

  1. Boil and mash the potatoes for the filling.
  2. Blend mint and coriander for the spicy water.
  3. Poke a hole in the puri shells.
  4. Fill the shells and dip them in the water!
