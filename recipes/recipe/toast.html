BREAD TOAST

Bread toast is perhaps the most fundamental breakfast dish in the world, valued for its simplicity and the satisfying crunch of maillard-browned bread. Whether it is a simple piece of white bread toasted in a machine or a thick slice of sourdough pan-fried in golden butter, toast serves as a versatile canvas for both sweet and savory toppings.

Beyond just butter, toast can be elevated in countless ways. In many households, a classic "Masala Toast" involves topping the bread with chopped onions, chilies, and spices before grilling. Others prefer the sweet simplicity of jam, honey, or a cinnamon-sugar dusting. From the trendy Avocado Toast to the humble French Toast, this crispy staple remains a favorite for its ability to be prepared in just a few minutes.

INGREDIENTS

  • Slices of bread (White, Brown, or Sourdough)
  • Unsalted or Salted Butter
  • Toppings (Jam, Honey, or Cheese)
  • Optional: A pinch of salt or cinnamon

STEPS

  1. Heat a skillet or pop the bread slices into a toaster.
  2. Toast until the bread reaches a golden-brown color on both sides.
  3. Immediately spread a generous layer of butter while the bread is still hot.
  4. Add your favorite toppings and serve while crunchy!
